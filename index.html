About

Growing up, it was either Medicine or Pharmacy. Watching the life story of Dr. Ben Carson in Gifted Hands, and seeing my older brother become a doctor, made me picture myself as a neurosurgeon. When I did not qualify for medical school, I pursued Petroleum Engineering at KNUST instead, a field I was not initially drawn to.

I struggled to find my footing at first. What changed things was a second-year project where every engineering student had to propose an idea using engineering principles to solve a societal problem. Working on a waste-to-energy concept let me draw from my lived experience, and for the first time, I connected what I was learning to the energy challenges I had grown up around. It marked the beginning of a longer curiosity.

After graduating, I worked in Ghana's upstream oil and gas sector, where I saw a country with significant natural gas resources still struggling to meet its own power demand. The engineering was rarely the constraint. Driven by a desire to contribute to the energy transition rather than just observe it, I pursued a Master's in Energy and Environment at Yale, focusing on energy systems, policy, project finance, and data science.

Since then, my work has focused on data-driven sustainability strategy across different contexts: battery storage market analysis, state decarbonization planning, sustainability program evaluation, and fleet decarbonization. I now work in utility solar operations and analytics at King Energy in California.

Outside of work, I serve as vocal director for my church choir. When I am not doing that, you will find me watching a movie or in a good conversation with friends.

I believe learning is a lifelong journey. Some of my most meaningful lessons have come from conversations, mentors, and unexpected experiences. I try to approach every opportunity with curiosity and a desire to leave things better than I found them. My faith in God is the foundation that shapes how I try to live and work.

Projects

Fleet Decarbonization Roadmap - BrandSafway - EDF Climate Corps

Developed a data-driven roadmap to reduce fuel consumption and GHG emissions across BrandSafway's North American fleet. Quantified ~$1.5M in potential cost avoidance and up to 3,400 MT CO₂ through behavioral, organizational, and technology analysis of telematics data.

Python Telematics Analysis GHG Accounting Fleet Analytics EDF Climate Corps

Chicago Solar Suitability Analysis

GIS-based analysis identifying the most solar-suitable neighborhoods in Chicago using rooftop potential, grid infrastructure, and demographic data. Found Belmont Cragin as the highest-priority corridor.

ArcGIS Pro GeoPandas Python Spatial Analysis

Solar Energy Equity & Data Science

County-level analysis identifying where high energy burden and solar potential overlap across the U.S., combining k-means clustering with spatial autocorrelation to understand patterns in solar adoption and socioeconomic need.

Python GeoPandas Spatial Autocorrelation K-Means Clustering

Mapping Methane Across the Okavango Delta

Used global FLUXNET tower data to train a Random Forest model predicting wetland methane emissions, then applied it spatially to map seasonal flux patterns across the Okavango Delta, one of Africa's largest and most carbon-significant wetland ecosystems.

R Random Forest Spatial Prediction FLUXNET
