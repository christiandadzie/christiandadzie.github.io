About

Growing up, it was either Medicine or Pharmacy. Watching the life story of Dr. Ben Carson in Gifted Hands, and seeing my older brother become a doctor, made me picture myself as a neurosurgeon. When I did not qualify for medical school, I pursued Petroleum Engineering at KNUST instead, a field I was not initially drawn to.

I struggled to find my footing at first. What changed things was a second-year project where every engineering student had to propose an idea using engineering principles to solve a societal problem. Working on a waste-to-energy concept let me draw from my lived experience, and for the first time, I connected what I was learning to the energy challenges I had grown up around. It marked the beginning of a longer curiosity.

After graduating, I worked in Ghana's upstream oil and gas sector, where I saw a country with significant natural gas resources still struggling to meet its own power demand. The engineering was rarely the constraint. Driven by a desire to contribute to the energy transition rather than just observe it, I pursued a Master's in Energy and Environment at Yale, focusing on energy systems, policy, project finance, and data science.

Since then, my work has focused on data-driven sustainability strategy across different contexts: battery storage market analysis, state decarbonization planning, sustainability program evaluation, and fleet decarbonization. I now work in utility solar operations and analytics at King Energy in California.

Outside of work, I serve as vocal director for my church choir. When I am not doing that, you will find me watching a movie or in a good conversation with friends.

I don't think anyone ever finishes learning. Some of my most meaningful lessons have come from conversations, mentors, and experiences I did not see coming. I try to approach every opportunity with curiosity and a desire to leave things better than I found them. My faith in God is the foundation that shapes how I try to live and work.
